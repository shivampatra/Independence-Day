Independence Day 2023 Theme :

In 2023, India going to celebrate the 77th anniversary of India's independence from British rule on Tuesday, August 15th and the Theme for Independence Day 2023 is "Nation First, Always First". It is a call to action for all Indians to put the nation first, regardless of their individual differences. The theme also reflects the government's commitment to building a stronger and more united India.

Independence Day History :

The British Empire ruled India for 150 years through the East India Company, the trading company that was set up in Surat, Gujarat in 1619. Post their victory at the Battle of Plassey, the East India Company, in 1757, took control of the nation.

Freedom fighters like Bhagat Singh, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, Subhas Chandra Bose, Chandra Shekhar Azad,Mahatma Gandhi , Jawaharlal Nehru and many others did their bid and fought for India's independence. In 1947, the Quit India Movement resulted in the British leaving the country.

After the freedom, the first Independence Day ceremony was held in New Delhi on August 15, 1947. The ceremony was presided over by the first Prime Minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru. He gave a speech in which he declared India to be a sovereign, democratic, republic.

Independence Day is a most important national holiday in the country that is celebrated by all Indians not just in the country but also around the world. It is celebrated with flag-hoisting ceremonies, parades, cultural programs, and fireworks. It is also a day for reflecting on the sacrifices that were made to achieve independence.

Independence Day 2023: Significance

Indian Independence Day is observed as a national holiday throughout the country. It reminds us of the many sacrifices our freedom fighters made for the freedom movement and to get Independence from British rule.

The country's history is written with many events of retaliation and uprisings led by the freedom fighters, eventually driving the Britishers out and forcing Lord Mountbatten, Viceroy of India at that time, to free the country from colonial rule on August 15, 1947. However, the day also marked the partition of British-ruled India into two countries - India and Pakistan.

Independence Day is marked with the hoisting of the tricolour or tiranga, parades, cultural events, and citizens singing patriotic songs. On August 15, 1947, the first prime minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru, raised the Indian national flag above the Lahori Gate of Red Fort in Delhi. It is a tradition that has since been followed by every Prime Minister with an address to the country.
